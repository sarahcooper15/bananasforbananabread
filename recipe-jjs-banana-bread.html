JJ's Banana Nut Bread

⏱ 1 hr 15 min 🍞 2 loaves 🏡 Athens, Alabama ⭐ Easy
Jump to Recipe ↓
About the Baker

Meet JJ — Jackie Johnson

Some recipes are more than instructions — they're a piece of someone's soul baked into every loaf. Jackie Johnson, lovingly known by everyone as JJ, was one of those rare people whose kitchen always smelled like warmth and whose table was always set with an extra plate, just in case.

Born and raised in Athens, Alabama, JJ spent decades perfecting this banana nut bread — a recipe she would go on to share not just with family, but with her entire community. Her handwritten note at the top of the card says it all: "easy, good — don't over cool." Simple, honest, and exactly right.

JJ's recipe was selected for the local Athens community cookbook, a testament to just how beloved it was by neighbors, church friends, and anyone lucky enough to visit her kitchen. Today, every loaf made from this recipe carries a little piece of her forward.

📖 Originally published in the Athens, AL Community Cookbook — as submitted by Mrs. William B. Warren

Step by Step

Follow along just the way JJ did it

Step 01
Cream the Sugar & Shortening

In a large mixing bowl, combine the 2 cups of sugar with your ½ cup of shortening and butter. Beat together until the mixture is pale, light, and fluffy — this is what gives the bread its tender crumb.

💡 Use softened (room-temperature) butter for the easiest creaming.
Step 02
Mash the Bananas

Peel your 3 ripe bananas and mash them thoroughly with a fork until smooth with just a few small lumps. The riper — the better. Spotted, almost-black bananas make the sweetest, most flavorful bread.

💡 Very ripe, almost black bananas are perfect — don't throw them away!
Step 03
Add the Remaining Ingredients

To the creamed mixture, add everything else: 1 cup milk, 2 eggs, mashed bananas, 2 tsp baking powder, 1 tsp baking soda, 3 cups flour, 1 tsp vanilla, and 1 cup chopped nuts. JJ put it all in together — no need to alternate.

💡 No need to sift the flour separately. JJ's method puts it all in at once.
Step 04
Beat for 3 Minutes

This is the key step. Beat the entire batter for a full 3 minutes on medium speed. This builds structure, incorporates air, and ensures everything is evenly combined into a smooth, thick batter.

💡 Set a timer — 3 minutes feels long but it really matters for the final texture.
Step 05
Grease & Flour the Pans

Take 2 standard loaf pans and grease them thoroughly with butter or shortening, coating the bottom and all sides. Dust each pan lightly with flour and tap out any excess. This ensures your bread releases cleanly after baking.

💡 Parchment paper on the bottom gives extra-easy release.
Step 06
Bake at 350°F for 1 Hour

Divide the batter evenly between your two prepared pans. Bake in a preheated 350°F oven for 1 full hour. The loaves are done when a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean and the tops are deep golden brown.

💡 Don't peek too often — opening the oven can cause the bread to sink.
Step 07
Cool & Serve

Rest in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack. As JJ always wrote: "don't over cool" — this bread is at its very best still just a little warm. Slice thick, add butter, and savor every bite.

💡 Wrap leftovers tightly in plastic wrap — stays moist 3–4 days on the counter.
