JJ's Sunday Biscuits

⏱ 30 min 🍽 12 biscuits 🏡 Athens, Alabama ⭐ Easy
Jump to Recipe ↓
About This Recipe

Sunday Morning, The Right Way

In JJ's house, Sunday morning had a smell. Before the coffee finished brewing and before anyone had found their shoes, the kitchen was already warm with the scent of butter and flour. JJ's biscuits weren't just breakfast — they were the whole reason to slow down and gather around the table.

Her method is deceptively simple: cold butter, cold buttermilk, and a hand that knows when to stop. The dough comes together in minutes, and the oven does the rest. What comes out are tall, golden, pull-apart biscuits with flaky layers that practically beg for a pat of honey butter.

🧈JJ's rule: if the butter isn't cold, the biscuits won't be flaky. Keep everything cold until the moment it hits the oven.

Step by Step

Follow along just the way JJ did it

Step 01
Preheat to 450°F

Position a rack in the upper third of your oven and preheat to 450°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. A hot oven is essential — it's what creates that dramatic rise and golden crust that makes these biscuits so special.

💡 Let the oven fully preheat for at least 15 minutes before baking.
Step 02
Whisk the Dry Ingredients

In a large bowl, whisk together 2 cups flour, 1 tbsp baking powder, 1 tsp salt, and ¼ tsp baking soda until well combined. Take your time here — even distribution of leaveners is what gives these biscuits their consistent rise.

💡 Measure flour by spooning it into the cup and leveling off — don't scoop directly from the bag.
Step 03
Cut in the Cold Butter

Add the 6 tbsp cold cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using your fingertips, quickly work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces remaining. Work fast — you want the butter to stay cold and not melt from your hands.

💡 Frozen butter grated on a box grater is JJ's secret for extra-flaky results.
Step 04
Add Buttermilk & Fold Gently

Pour in the ¾ cup cold buttermilk all at once. Using a fork or spatula, fold the dough together with as few strokes as possible — just until no dry flour remains. The dough will be shaggy and imperfect. That's perfect. Overworking it kills the flake.

💡 Stop mixing the moment it comes together. Lumpy dough = flaky biscuits.
Step 05
Pat Out & Cut

Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently pat (don't roll) to 1-inch thickness. Use a sharp biscuit cutter pressed straight down — no twisting. Twisting seals the edges and prevents a proper rise. Re-pat scraps once and cut remaining biscuits.

💡 Place biscuits touching each other on the baking sheet — they'll rise taller by supporting each other.
Step 06
Bake 12–14 Minutes

Bake in the preheated 450°F oven for 12–14 minutes until the tops are beautifully golden brown. Brush with melted butter the moment they come out. Serve immediately — hot biscuits are one of life's genuine pleasures.

💡 Don't over-bake. Pull them when golden — they'll continue to set as they rest for 2 minutes.
