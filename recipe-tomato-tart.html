Momma's Signature Tomato Tart

⏱ 45 min 🍽 6 servings 🥗 Healthy ⭐ Easy
Jump to Recipe ↓
About This Recipe

Elegant. Effortless. Unmistakably Momma.

This tart is Momma's go-to for dinner guests — the kind of dish that looks like you spent hours in the kitchen but actually comes together in under 45 minutes. The combination of golden puff pastry, sharp Dijon, creamy mozzarella, and peak-season heirloom tomatoes is nothing short of perfect.

It's also remarkably healthy for how indulgent it tastes. No heavy cream, no complicated technique — just high-quality ingredients treated simply. Momma has always believed that the best cooking is about knowing when to step back and let the ingredients speak for themselves. This tart is proof of that philosophy.

🍅This recipe is only as good as your tomatoes. Use the best, ripest heirloom tomatoes you can find — peak summer is the perfect time to make this.

Serves 6  ·  Prep: 15 min  ·  Bake: 25–30 min at 400°F

Step by Step

Simple technique, spectacular result

Step 01
Prep the Pastry

Preheat oven to 400°F. Unfold the thawed puff pastry sheet onto a parchment-lined baking sheet. Using a sharp knife, score a 1-inch border around all four edges — cut only halfway through, not all the way. This border will puff up into a beautiful golden frame.

💡 Chill the pastry for 10 minutes after scoring — a cold pastry puffs better in the oven.
Step 02
Spread the Dijon Base

Spread 2 tbsp Dijon mustard in an even layer inside the scored border. The mustard acts as both glue and flavor base — it keeps the tomatoes from making the pastry soggy and adds a subtle, sophisticated bite that makes the whole tart sing.

💡 Whole-grain Dijon adds even more flavor and texture. Either works beautifully.
Step 03
Layer the Mozzarella

Arrange slices of fresh mozzarella in an even layer over the mustard, staying inside the scored border. Overlap them slightly. Fresh mozzarella is key here — it melts into creamy pools that cradle the tomatoes perfectly as everything bakes together.

💡 Pat the mozzarella dry with a paper towel to reduce excess moisture.
Step 04
Arrange the Tomatoes

Slice your heirloom tomatoes about ¼-inch thick. Arrange them in overlapping rows or concentric circles over the mozzarella — go edge to edge within the border. Use a mix of colors if you can find them: red, yellow, and purple heirloom tomatoes make this tart genuinely stunning.

💡 Blot the tomato slices gently — less moisture means a crispier crust.
Step 05
Season, Egg Wash & Bake

Drizzle 2 tbsp olive oil over the tomatoes, then season generously with flaky sea salt and black pepper. Brush the exposed border with egg wash for a beautiful golden sheen. Bake at 400°F for 25–30 minutes until the crust is deeply golden and puffed.

💡 Watch the edges — they can darken quickly in the last 5 minutes. Tent with foil if needed.
Step 06
Finish with Fresh Basil & Serve

The moment the tart comes out of the oven, scatter fresh basil leaves generously over the top. The heat will gently wilt them and release their fragrance. Slice and serve warm — this is when it's at its absolute best. A drizzle of good olive oil on each slice is a perfect finishing touch.

💡 This tart also holds up well at room temperature — great for dinner parties or picnics.
