Braised Beef and Stilton Pie

⏱ 4+ hrs 🍽 8–10 servings 🥘 Hearty 🌙 Full Crow Moon
Jump to Recipe ↓
About This Recipe

Individual Pies Worth Every Minute.

This recipe has only a top crust and is thus best suited for servings in individual crocks — you will need eight to ten ovenproof crocks. As part of a multicourse meal, a meat pie with a double crust might be too filling. But if the meat pie is being served alone, by all means, double the crust recipe and surround the filling with buttery pastry!

The beef braises low and slow in Guinness stout and rich beef stock until fork-tender, absorbing the deep, malty flavor of the stout along the way. A bold crumble of Stilton on top before sealing with pastry adds a salty, funky punch that makes this pie genuinely unforgettable. It's the kind of dish that fills the whole house with the most incredible smell while it cooks.

🍺The Guinness is non-negotiable — it's the backbone of the braising liquid. The bitterness mellows beautifully over hours of cooking into a rich, almost chocolatey depth.

For the Beef Stew

2 tbspOlive oil
3 lbsStew beef (shoulder or rump), cut into 1-inch cubes
To tasteKosher salt & fresh ground pepper
As neededFlour for dredging
2 largeOnions, diced
8Carrots, cut into 1-inch diagonal pieces
8Celery ribs, cut into 1-inch diagonal pieces
4 clovesGarlic, peeled and minced
2 tbspTomato paste
Few shakesWorcestershire sauce
1 tbspDijon mustard
1Bouquet garni (1 large sprig thyme, 1 sprig rosemary, 2 parsley stems, 1 bay leaf — tied together)
1 lbYellow potatoes, peeled and chopped into ½-inch cubes
8 cupsBeef stock
One 14.9-oz canGuinness stout
1 cupFrozen peas
2 tbspButter
HandfulFresh parsley leaves

For the Stilton Topping & Crust

6 ozStilton cheese, crumbled
3 cupsAll-purpose flour
1 tspSalt
1 cup (2 sticks)Cold butter, cut into small pieces
~3 tbspIce water

Step by Step

Low and slow — this one rewards patience

Step 01
Brown the Beef

In a large stewing pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Season the beef cubes with salt and pepper, dredge them in flour, and brown them in batches, using a set of tongs to transfer each piece to a plate when done. Don't overcrowd the pot — the beef will steam rather than brown.

💡 A deep golden-brown crust on the meat is where much of the flavor develops. Take your time with this step.
Step 02
Sweat the Vegetables & Build the Base

Sweat the onions, carrots, and celery in the stew pot until soft. Add the garlic and continue cooking until the vegetables start to gain color. Mush the tomato paste into the vegetables; cook 2–3 minutes until it darkens. Add the Worcestershire sauce and Dijon mustard and stir to fully incorporate.

💡 Let the tomato paste caramelize — that extra 2 minutes of color adds incredible depth to the final stew.
Step 03
Braise Low and Slow

Return the browned beef to the pot. Add the bouquet garni, potatoes, 8 cups beef stock, and Guinness stout. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer, cover, and cook for at least 2½ hours and up to 3 hours, until the beef is very tender. Taste and season. Discard the bouquet garni.

💡 The stew should be thick with enough liquid that it won't dry out in the oven. If it seems too thick, add ½ cup of water.
Step 04
Finish the Stew & Make the Crust

Add the frozen peas and cook for 2 minutes. Swirl in the butter and sprinkle with parsley leaves. Let cool completely. Meanwhile, make the crust: combine flour and salt in a bowl. Cut in the cold butter with a pastry cutter until it resembles coarse cornmeal. Add ~3 tbsp ice water until the dough just holds together. Shape into two equal balls, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for 20 minutes.

💡 The stew must be fully cooled before assembling — hot filling will melt the crust before it reaches the oven.
Step 05
Assemble the Individual Pies

Preheat the oven to 400°F. Ladle the cooled stew into 8–10 individual ovenproof crocks, filling to just below the rim. Crumble a generous amount of Stilton over each crock. Roll the chilled dough out and cut circles to fit over each crock. Drape the pastry over, press the edges to seal, and cut a small vent slit in the top.

💡 Brush the pastry top with egg wash for a beautiful deep golden glaze in the oven.
Step 06
Bake Until Golden & Serve

Bake at 400°F for 25–35 minutes, until the pastry is deeply golden and the filling is bubbling up through the vents. Let the crocks rest for 5 minutes before serving — they will be extremely hot. Serve directly in the crocks with crusty bread for soaking up every last drop of that Guinness braise.

💡 The Stilton melts into the filling as it bakes — you'll get pockets of rich, salty cheese in every bite.
