Simple Roasted Chicken

⏱ 1 hour 🍗 ~6 cups shredded 🌿 Paleo · Whole30 ⭐ Easy
Jump to Recipe ↓
About This Recipe

The Meal-Prep Staple That Changes Everything

Every home cook needs a reliable go-to for shredded chicken — the kind that comes together in one hour, keeps well all week, and slides effortlessly into soups, salads, grain bowls, and anything else that calls for it. This is ours. Bone-in, skin-on pieces roast on a single sheet pan at 375°F, giving the meat time to stay juicy while developing just enough golden color.

Because breasts and thighs cook at different rates, the technique staggers them: thighs come out first after 35 minutes, the breasts finish alone for another 10. The result is properly cooked chicken throughout — no dry breast meat, no undercooked thighs. Once rested and shredded, you'll have around 6 cups ready to go for whatever the week throws at you. You can also substitute a store-bought rotisserie chicken if you're short on time.

Ingredients

Makes ~6 cups shredded  ·  Prep: 5 min  ·  Roast: ~45 min at 375°F

4 (about 2½ lbs)Bone-in, skin-on chicken breasts
2 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
4 (about 1½ lbs)Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
2 tspKosher salt
1 tspFreshly ground black pepper

Step by Step

One pan, one hour, a week's worth of protein

Step 01
Preheat & Prep the Pan

Heat your oven to 375°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper — this makes cleanup easy and prevents sticking.

💡 A rimmed sheet pan is important here so any juices stay contained while the chicken roasts.
Step 02
Arrange, Oil & Season

Place the chicken breasts and thighs on the parchment in a single layer. Drizzle with 2 tablespoons of olive oil and rub each piece all over to coat evenly. Sprinkle the tops with 2 tsp kosher salt and 1 tsp black pepper.

💡 Make sure pieces aren't touching — spacing allows hot air to circulate and the skin to color evenly.
Step 03
Roast the Thighs

Slide the pan into the preheated oven and roast until the thighs are fully cooked through, about 35 minutes. The thighs finish first because their smaller size and higher fat content cook faster than the thick breasts.

💡 To check doneness, pierce the thickest part of a thigh — the juices should run clear with no pink.
Step 04
Stagger the Breasts

Using tongs, carefully transfer the cooked thighs to a cutting board to rest. Return the sheet pan with the breasts back to the oven and continue roasting for about 10 more minutes, or until the meat is no longer pink and the juices run clear.

💡 This two-stage approach is the key to the recipe — it ensures moist breasts and fully cooked thighs every single time.
Step 05
Rest, Peel & Shred

Let all the chicken rest for about 10 minutes — this allows the juices to redistribute before you break into the meat. Once cool enough to handle, peel off and discard the skin, then use your hands (or two forks) to pull the meat into bite-size shredded pieces.

💡 Store shredded chicken in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days — or freeze in portions for up to 3 months.
