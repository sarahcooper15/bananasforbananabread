Baked Blueberry French Toast

⏱ 40 min bake + overnight soak 🍽 10 servings 🫐 Breakfast ⭐ Easy
Jump to Recipe ↓
About This Recipe

The Make-Ahead Brunch Secret

This baked French toast is the kind of recipe that earns its place in the family rotation — mostly because all the hard work happens the night before. You soak thick-cut slices of dry French bread in a simple vanilla egg custard, tuck it in the fridge, and wake up to the easiest baked breakfast imaginable.

What makes it truly special is the streusel: a buttery, crumbly mix of flour, dark brown sugar, and cinnamon that bakes on top of a generous layer of blueberries. The result is golden, slightly crispy on top, custardy underneath, and bursting with fruit in every bite.

💙Fresh or frozen blueberries both work beautifully — no need to thaw frozen ones first.

Ingredients

Makes 10 (2½×4½-inch) slice servings  ·  Prep: 20 min + 8–10 hr chill  ·  Bake: 40 min

1 (9-oz) loafDry French bread
½ cupAll-purpose flour
3 largeEggs
6 tbspDark brown sugar
3 tbspSugar
½ tspGround cinnamon
1 tspVanilla extract
¼ cup (½ stick)Butter or margarine
2¼ cupsMilk
1 cupFresh or frozen blueberries

Step by Step

Start the night before for an effortless morning bake

Step 01
Arrange the Bread

Cut the 9-oz loaf of dry French bread diagonally into 1-inch slices. Arrange them cut side down in a single layer in a greased 9×13-inch baking dish. Using day-old or dried-out bread is key — it soaks up the custard without turning to mush.

💡 Leave the bread out uncovered overnight beforehand if it isn't already dry — stale bread is the goal here.
Step 02
Make the Custard & Chill

Beat the 3 eggs, 3 tbsp sugar, and 1 tsp vanilla in a mixing bowl until smooth. Stir in the 2¼ cups of milk until fully blended. Pour over the bread slices, turning each slice to coat. Cover and refrigerate for 8 to 10 hours (overnight is perfect).

💡 Turning the slices to coat both sides ensures even absorption. Make sure every slice gets soaked.
Step 03
Make the Streusel

When ready to bake, preheat the oven to 375°F. In a bowl, mix together the ½ cup flour, 6 tbsp dark brown sugar, and ½ tsp cinnamon. Cut in the ¼ cup (½ stick) butter or margarine until the mixture is crumbly and resembles coarse crumbs — this is your streusel topping.

💡 Use your fingertips or a pastry cutter to work the butter in quickly. You want clumps, not a smooth mixture.
Step 04
Top & Bake

Remove the soaked bread from the fridge. Turn the slices over in the dish. Sprinkle the 1 cup of blueberries evenly over the top, followed by the streusel. Bake for 40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the custard is set.

💡 Don't thaw frozen blueberries before using — add them straight from frozen so they don't bleed into the topping.
Step 05
Cut Into Squares & Serve

Once golden brown and puffed, remove from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. Cut into squares to serve — you'll get about 10 (2½×4½-inch) servings. The streusel should be crispy, the blueberries jammy, and the custard perfectly set.

💡 Serve warm, straight from the dish. A drizzle of maple syrup or a dusting of powdered sugar takes it over the top.
