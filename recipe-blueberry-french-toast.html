Momma's Blueberry French Toast

⏱ 25 min 🍽 4 servings 🫐 Breakfast ⭐ Easy
Jump to Recipe ↓
About This Recipe

A Weekend Morning Ritual

If there's one breakfast that signals a real weekend morning in the Cooper household, it's this one. Momma's blueberry French toast has been the centerpiece of lazy Saturday mornings and holiday brunches for as long as anyone can remember.

The secret is the brioche — thick slices that soak up the vanilla-cinnamon custard without falling apart — and the blueberry maple syrup that she simmers fresh on the stovetop while the toast cooks. It comes together in about 25 minutes, but it feels like something you'd order at a fancy brunch spot.

💙Use the freshest blueberries you can find. Frozen work in a pinch but fresh is worth it.

Step by Step

Follow along just the way Momma does it

Step 01
Make the Custard

In a wide, shallow bowl, whisk together the 4 eggs, ¾ cup milk, 1 tsp vanilla, 1 tsp cinnamon, and a pinch of salt until completely smooth and uniform. The custard should be golden yellow with no streaks of white.

💡 A shallow bowl or pie dish makes dipping the brioche much easier than a deep bowl.
Step 02
Make the Blueberry Syrup

In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine the 2 cups of blueberries and 3 tbsp maple syrup. Stir and cook for about 5–7 minutes until the berries burst and the mixture becomes thick and jammy. Remove from heat and set aside.

💡 A splash of lemon juice brightens the syrup beautifully — totally optional but worth it.
Step 03
Soak the Brioche

Dip each thick slice of brioche into the custard, letting each side soak for a full 30 seconds. The bread should be well-saturated but not falling apart. Brioche holds up beautifully to a proper soak — don't rush this step.

💡 Day-old brioche soaks more evenly and doesn't get soggy. Slightly stale is ideal.
Step 04
Cook Until Golden

Melt 1 tbsp butter in a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Cook soaked brioche slices for 2–3 minutes per side until deeply golden brown. Work in batches, adding more butter as needed. The custard should be fully set with no raw egg.

💡 Medium heat is key — too high and the outside burns before the inside sets.
Step 05
Stack, Top & Serve

Stack two or three slices on a warm plate. Spoon the warm blueberry maple syrup generously over the top, letting it cascade down the sides. Finish with a dusting of powdered sugar. Serve immediately — French toast waits for no one.

💡 A sprig of fresh mint makes this look like something from a restaurant. Completely optional, completely worth it.
