Momma's Christmas Gift Chex Mix

⏱ 1 hr 15 min 🎄 ~14 cups 🎁 Great for gifting ⭐ Easy
Jump to Recipe ↓
About This Recipe

The Gift Everyone Hopes They're Getting

Every December, without fail, Momma's kitchen fills with the warm, savory scent of slow-baking Chex Mix. It's one of those smells that means the holidays have officially arrived. She's been making this recipe for longer than most of us can remember — and giving it away in tins has become as much a part of Christmas as the tree itself.

The secret is the slow bake at low heat. It's not fast, but it's worth every minute — each piece gets evenly coated, perfectly crispy, and deeply seasoned in a way that oven-bag versions simply can't match. Make a double batch. You'll thank yourself when December gets busy.

🎁Pack in holiday tins or Mason jars tied with ribbon — it's the most appreciated food gift our family gives.

Ingredients

Makes ~14 cups  ·  Prep: 15 min  ·  Bake: 1 hour at 250°F

3 cupsCorn Chex
6 tbspButter
3 cupsWheat Chex
4 tbspWorcestershire sauce
3 cupsRice Chex
1½ tspSeasoned salt
1 cupMixed nuts
¾ tspGarlic powder
1 cupBite-size pretzels
½ tspOnion powder

Step by Step

Low and slow is the only way

Step 01
Preheat to 250°F

Preheat your oven to a low 250°F. This is the key to Momma's recipe — the low temperature slowly toasts everything to an even, deep crunch without burning. Get a large roasting pan or rimmed baking sheet ready.

💡 A large roasting pan works best — you need room to stir without spilling.
Step 02
Make the Butter Sauce

Melt 6 tbsp butter in a small saucepan over low heat. Stir in the 4 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1½ tsp seasoned salt, ¾ tsp garlic powder, and ½ tsp onion powder. Stir until combined and fragrant. Remove from heat.

💡 Don't rush this step — the seasonings need to bloom in the warm butter for full flavor.
Step 03
Combine the Dry Mix

In your large roasting pan, combine the 3 cups each of Corn, Wheat, and Rice Chex, the 1 cup mixed nuts, and 1 cup pretzels. Toss together so all the pieces are evenly distributed across the pan before adding the sauce.

💡 A large disposable roasting pan makes cleanup easy and gives plenty of room to stir.
Step 04
Coat with Butter Sauce

Drizzle the warm butter sauce evenly over the entire dry mix, a little at a time. After each addition, gently toss to coat everything. This gradual approach ensures every single piece gets seasoned — no dry spots, no over-saturated clumps.

💡 Use a large spoon or rubber spatula and be gentle — you want the Chex to stay whole.
Step 05
Slow Bake 1 Hour, Stir Every 15 Min

Bake uncovered at 250°F for 1 full hour, gently stirring every 15 minutes to ensure even toasting. The mix will transform from soft and buttery to deeply crispy and fragrant. Your kitchen will smell incredible — that's how you know it's working.

💡 Set four 15-minute timers so you don't forget a stir. Each stir matters for even crunch.
Step 06
Cool & Store (or Gift!)

Spread the hot mix onto paper towels and let it cool completely — at least 30 minutes. It will crisp up further as it cools. Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks. Pack into holiday tins or Mason jars for gifting — it's the gift everyone hopes they're getting.

💡 Do not store until fully cooled — trapping steam makes it go soft.
